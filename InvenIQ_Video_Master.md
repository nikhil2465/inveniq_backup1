# SEGMENT 1 — THE OPENING (0:00 – 1:45)

Every business in the hardware, sanitary ware, and building materials industry is managing the same hidden problem.

Sales quotations are tracked in WhatsApp message threads. Purchase orders are managed in Excel files that only one person can access at a time. Inventory counts happen manually once a week or once a month — and the numbers are outdated the moment they are written down. Customer follow-ups depend entirely on whoever happens to remember to call. And at the end of every month, the finance team spends three to five days pulling together reports from disconnected sources to understand what actually happened to the business.

This fragmentation is not a minor inconvenience. It costs money every single month. Dead stock accumulates in warehouses because no one is continuously monitoring what is not selling. Quotations expire and deals are lost because follow-ups are inconsistent. Payments are made to suppliers for goods that were partially rejected on quality grounds. GST is applied incorrectly on interstate transactions. Management decisions are made on data that is weeks out of date.

InvenIQ was built to solve exactly this.

This is a complete AI-powered business operating system — designed specifically for distributors, interior designers, and B2B sales teams in hardware, sanitary ware, and building materials. It consolidates every part of a business operation — procurement, inventory, sales, quotations, design projects, finance, and customer management — into a single platform. And it adds an artificial intelligence layer that works around the clock, analysing live data and delivering actionable intelligence on demand, in plain English, without requiring any technical knowledge from the user.

What makes InvenIQ different from every other software available for this industry is not simply the number of modules or features. It is the depth of integration between operational workflows and AI intelligence. Every screen, every data point, every module — the AI is present and connected. The platform was not built by adapting a generic ERP for this industry. It was built from the ground up for hardware, sanitary ware, tiles, plumbing, and building materials — and it speaks the operational language of this industry fluently.

# SEGMENT 3 — INVENTORY INTELLIGENCE (3:45 – 5:45)

InvenIQ's inventory management gives complete SKU-level visibility across the entire product catalog. Every item shows current stock quantity, reorder point, days of stock remaining at current sales velocity, supplier linkage, category classification, and HSN code. The system continuously monitors stock levels against reorder thresholds and surfaces alerts before stockouts occur — giving the purchase team time to act before the sales team runs into a problem.

The platform tracks every stock movement automatically: from purchase receiving at the warehouse dock to sales dispatch to returns processing to damage recording. Shrinkage is detected when inward records and outward records do not reconcile. Put-away locations are tracked per warehouse zone. Dispatch velocity is measured against committed delivery timelines.

The Warehouse Management module extends this visibility across multiple locations — tracking capacity utilisation by zone, GRN activity log, and stock distribution analysis across all warehouse locations. For businesses operating from more than one warehouse, consolidated stock across all locations is always visible from a single screen.

The Demand Forecasting module uses historical sales patterns, seasonal adjustments, and current order pipeline data to project future demand by SKU and category. Procurement teams use this to determine optimal reorder quantities and reduce both stockout risk and overstock accumulation simultaneously.

The Dead Stock and Ageing module is one of the most commercially impactful screens in the entire platform.

In every distribution and trading business, a significant portion of working capital is locked up in inventory that is not moving. Products purchased months ago are occupying warehouse space and generating zero revenue. Most businesses only discover the true extent of this problem during an annual physical audit — by which point the capital has been locked for months.

InvenIQ identifies every slow-moving and non-moving item automatically — continuously. It calculates the exact rupee value of capital locked in idle inventory, categorises the problem by product type, age bracket, and category, and through the AI, recommends specific clearance strategies: structured discounting to channel partners, product bundling with fast-moving items, supplier return negotiation, or inter-location transfer to a location where the item sells.

The AI cross-references purchase date, last sale date, current quantity, and demand patterns — and returns a prioritised action plan. For most businesses, a thorough review of the Dead Stock screen identifies lakhs of rupees in reclaimable working capital that had been invisible in the weekly inventory report.

A hardware distributor with 200 active SKUs typically has 15 to 25 percent of inventory value in slow-moving or non-moving stock. InvenIQ's continuous monitoring and AI-recommended clearance strategies help businesses recover eight to twelve lakhs in working capital in the first three months of operation.

# SEGMENT 4 — THE QUOTATION BUILDER (5:45 – 9:15)

The Quotation Builder is the most-used module in InvenIQ for B2B hardware and sanitary ware businesses. It is where revenue is created — managing every sales quotation for hardware, sanitary ware, plumbing, tiles, and building materials from creation through to closure.

The hero section at the top of the Quotation Builder shows seven live key performance indicators simultaneously. Total pipeline value across all active quotes. Revenue won year-to-date. Value lost in the same period. Current win rate percentage. Average gross margin across the active portfolio. Average deal size. And the number of quotations expiring within the next seven days that need urgent follow-up. Every single tile is clickable and opens the AI with a contextual question.

The sales pipeline is structured and measurable. Every quotation moves through defined stages: Draft, Sent to Customer, Negotiating, Won, Lost, or Expired. A status strip shows live counts for every stage. One click on any stage filters the entire quotation table to that stage instantly. A sales manager can assess the health of the complete pipeline in under one minute.

Creating a professional, GST-ready quotation takes under two minutes. The form captures customer details, project name, validity date, and GST rate. Line items are added from the product catalog with one-click selection. For each line item, the salesperson enters quantity and discount percentage. The system calculates the unit price from the catalog, computes the line margin in real time, and shows a colour-coded margin health bar — green for healthy, amber for marginal, red for below threshold. A deal score at the top of the form gives an immediate read on whether this quotation is commercially sound.

The quotation is formatted for professional printing and email delivery automatically, with company letterhead, itemised product list with HSN codes, quantity, rate, discount, GST breakdown, and total payable. It is ready to share with the customer the moment it is created.

For active quotations, every common action is available directly in the table row without opening the full record. A SENT quotation can be moved to Negotiating with one click when the customer responds. Won and Lost status updates are one-click operations that update the pipeline for the entire team in real time.

The most commercially impactful feature of the Quotation Builder is the per-quote AI assistant. Every quotation row has a dedicated AI button. Clicking it on a SENT quotation that has not received a response generates a personalised WhatsApp follow-up message — with the customer's name, the specific quote amount, product details, a professional and conversational tone, and a direct push toward closing the deal. The salesperson copies this, pastes it into WhatsApp, and sends it in under ten seconds.

For a sales team managing 40 to 60 active quotations at any time, this is the difference between consistent follow-up and deals falling through the cracks. Businesses using the AI-generated follow-up messages report a 20 to 35 percent improvement in quotation response rates within the first month, because follow-ups are sent consistently at the right time rather than whenever someone remembers to do it.

The multi-image product search allows salespeople to photograph unknown products from a site visit or a customer reference, upload multiple photos simultaneously, and receive AI identification of each product — brand name, category, HSN code, and catalog match. A two-panel interface shows the uploaded images alongside the AI identification results for each. This is particularly useful for matching competitor products or identifying items from old site photographs where no spec sheet is available.

The WhatsApp requirement scanner reads a customer's WhatsApp message — even a free-form, informal description of what they need — and extracts a structured product list with quantities, inferred brands, and HSN codes. The extracted list populates the quotation line items directly. An informal message saying "need 10 Jaquar single lever for kitchen, 5 pieces shower set premium range, 2 washbasins with pedestal" becomes a formatted, HSN-coded quotation line list in under five seconds.

The Kanban view provides a visual pipeline board with each quotation as a card organised by stage. Sales managers use this view for pipeline review meetings to quickly identify bottlenecks and stalled deals.

# SEGMENT 5 — THE DESIGN QUOTE STUDIO (9:15 – 12:45)

The Design Quote Studio is a dedicated module for interior fit-out businesses, architects, and businesses that supply hardware and sanitary ware to residential and commercial fit-out projects. Interior fit-out projects have a completely different quoting workflow from standard distribution sales. A project for a 3BHK apartment involves dozens of product categories, room-wise bills of quantities, architect fee structures, multi-level client approvals, and client sign-offs. None of that fits into a standard sales quotation. The Design Quote Studio was built specifically for that workflow.

The module has two distinct workflows managed from a single interface: Interior Quotations and Architect Fee Proposals.

Interior Quotations are room-wise bills of quantities for fit-out projects. A typical project for a 24-unit residential development — such as Brigade Lakefront — has BOQ sections for each bathroom type: Master Bathroom, Common Bathroom, Powder Room, and Utility Area. Within each section, products are listed with their item type classification. The item type system covers the complete vocabulary of hardware and sanitary fit-out: CP fittings, sanitary ware, bathroom accessories, hardware hinges, hardware channels, hardware handles, hardware locks, plumbing, tiles, waterproofing membrane, and installation. Each item carries quantity, unit, rate, and GST at 18 percent under SAC code 998331 as applicable to design and fit-out services.

Architect Fee Proposals follow the standard six-phase project billing structure used across the industry: Concept Design, Schematic Design, Design Development, Construction Documents, Bidding and Negotiation, and Site Supervision. For each phase, the fee percentage and deliverables are specified. The total architect fee typically ranges from five to eight percent of total project cost. Each phase can be approved and invoiced independently as the project progresses, giving both the architect and the client clarity on payment milestones.

The pipeline KPIs in Design Quote Studio track design project value completely separately from product distribution sales — total pipeline, approved value, active projects, and expired quotes. The business owner and CFO can see the design revenue stream in isolation from the trading business at any time.

Design quotations go through a structured multi-level approval chain within the application. The architect or designer creates and submits a quotation. The sales manager reviews and approves at Level 1. For projects above a configurable value threshold, the approval escalates to the CFO at Level 2, and to the business owner at Level 3. Every approval level is handled within the application — no email chains, no WhatsApp forwards, no printed approval forms with physical signatures. Approvers can approve, escalate, or return a quote to draft directly from the table row without opening the full record.

The AI WhatsApp Scanner in Design Quote Studio is the feature that most immediately demonstrates why this platform is different from anything else available in this market.

An architect or site supervisor visits a project location. They photograph the bathrooms, the kitchen, the living spaces. Or they receive a WhatsApp message from the client describing what they want — brand preferences, finishes, quantities for each bathroom type across all units in a residential development. The AI reads the images or the text message and automatically identifies every product: Jaquar Flora basin mixer, Hettich Quadro soft-close channels for the kitchen, Nitco 600×600 polished porcelain floor tiles, Pidilite Dr. Fixit waterproofing membrane for the wet areas. For each item, the AI extracts the quantity, brand reference, inferred HSN code, item type category, number of units, and number of bathrooms per unit for multi-unit residential projects.

The result is a complete, structured, room-wise BOQ — ready for client review and sign-off. Work that previously required an experienced designer two to three hours is completed in under one minute. For a business handling five to ten active design projects simultaneously, the time saving across a month is enormous.

The multi-image visual search in Design Quote Studio uses the same two-panel interface as the Quotation Builder. Multiple photographs — from a mood board, a specification sheet, a competitor installation — are processed simultaneously, with the AI returning brand, model, category, and catalog match for each visible product.

The QB to DQB sync feature — Quotation Builder to Design Quote Builder sync — allows a sales quotation created for a standard distribution sale to be imported directly into Design Quote Studio with one click. This is useful when the sales team creates an initial product list and then passes it to the design team for development into a full room-wise BOQ.

The Design Quote Studio has a dedicated user role: Architect. An external architect partner can be given system access that is limited exclusively to the Design Quote Studio, Settings, and About pages. They can manage their own projects and quotations within the system without having any visibility into the company's internal trading data, customer base, pricing, or financial information.

# SEGMENT 6 — PROCUREMENT AND P2P CYCLE (12:45 – 14:45)

For businesses managing their own procurement, InvenIQ includes a complete procure-to-pay workflow — from the initial internal request through to supplier payment. The five modules in this workflow are tightly integrated with each other and with the inventory system, ensuring complete traceability from the moment a requirement is identified to the moment payment is released.

The cycle begins with the Purchase Requisition module. Any department — warehouse, operations, sales — can raise a PR specifying what is needed, the quantity, the required delivery date, and the priority level. The PR goes through a configurable approval chain before being converted into a Purchase Order. A single PR can be split across multiple suppliers — one supplier handling CP fittings, another handling tiles — and the system tracks partial conversion per line item, maintaining a clear record of what has been ordered and what is still outstanding.

When the PR is approved, it is converted into one or more Purchase Orders with one click. The PO records the supplier, items ordered, agreed rates, expected delivery date, and any rate negotiation notes. The system prevents duplicate PO creation: a single draft PO per supplier per PR is enforced at the database level.

When goods arrive, a Goods Receipt Note is created against the PO. The system enforces receiving controls — a GRN cannot be raised against a PO that is already fully received, closed, cancelled, or returned. This single enforcement rule prevents an entire category of procurement errors: duplicate receiving, paying for goods that were never received, and over-receiving against partial orders.

After the GRN is recorded, goods go through Quality Control inspection. The QC Inspection module handles this with four outcome categories: Accepted, Rejected (returned to vendor), Rework Required, and Hold for Further Review. Rejected quantities are automatically deducted from available inventory and create a debit note entry against the supplier. Rework items are tracked separately until they are either accepted or rejected. The system maintains a cumulative quality scorecard per supplier, identifying suppliers with consistently high rejection rates.

When the supplier invoice arrives, the Invoice Matching module performs automated 3-way matching: comparing the Purchase Order quantity and rate, the Goods Receipt Note received quantity, and the supplier invoice amounts. Discrepancies within a one percent tolerance are auto-approved. Larger discrepancies are flagged for manual review with a per-line breakdown showing exactly where the numbers diverge. Payment cannot be approved if the QC inspection is still pending for that GRN — ensuring that no payment leaves the business before the quality outcome is determined.

The system generates journal entries automatically at each stage: a stock receipt entry at GRN, a QC rejection entry, and an accounts payable entry when the invoice is matched. Finance teams have complete accounting traceability from requisition to payment without any manual journal entry.

# SEGMENT 7 — THE AI ASSISTANT (14:45 – 17:45)

The AI Assistant is the intelligence layer of InvenIQ. It is not a chatbot that answers FAQ questions. It is not a keyword search tool. It is a business analyst that has read and understood every record in the live database — every inventory entry, every sale, every purchase order, every quotation, every customer interaction, every invoice — and can answer any question about the business in plain English, in real time, with data-driven recommendations.

The assistant operates across three modes. Ask mode delivers quick answers: current stock levels, overdue customer balances, quotation status, supplier lead times. The answer arrives in seconds. Explain mode delivers deep analysis with root cause identification: why dead stock has accumulated in a specific category over the past quarter, why a supplier's quality rejection rate has increased, why a product segment is showing declining margins despite growing volumes. Act mode delivers step-by-step execution plans: a structured clearance strategy for a specific set of dead stock items, a collection escalation plan for a specific overdue customer, a procurement optimisation plan for the next quarter.

The AI has 28 live data tools that it selects from based on the nature of the question. When asked about customer collection, it uses the customer tool and the finance tool simultaneously. When asked about procurement health, it combines the supplier tool, the PO and GRN tool, and the purchase requisition tool. When asked about the design project pipeline, it uses the design quote tool alongside the projects tool and the catalog tool. The selection is automatic — the user simply types a question.

Questions that demonstrate this capability most clearly:

"Which customers have not placed an order in the last 60 days and what is the best approach to re-engage them?" — The AI queries live customer, order, and sales data simultaneously and returns a prioritised list of inactive customers with a specific re-engagement message for each, based on the customer's purchase history, outstanding balance, and segment.

"What is my total dead stock value, which product categories are worst, and what is your recommended clearance strategy?" — The AI cross-references inventory records, purchase dates, sale history, and category data. It returns the total capital locked, a breakdown by category, and a specific clearance recommendation per category based on market demand patterns.

"Summarise my quotation pipeline — total value by status, average deal size, and which deals are most likely to close this month." — The AI analyses all active quotations and returns a pipeline summary, identifies the deals showing the strongest buying signals, and flags which ones need immediate follow-up action.

The knowledge base contains 36 topics covering industry-specific expertise: GST on design services, Tally integration best practices, credit management for distributors, the complete hardware and sanitary product universe, landing cost calculation, and detailed explanations of every InvenIQ workflow. Users can ask conceptual questions — "how does the 3-way matching work?" — and receive precise explanations.

The proactive intelligence engine runs 26 analytical queries continuously and surfaces insights automatically without the user having to ask. These 26 intelligence engines cover: demand anomaly detection when a high-velocity product's sales suddenly drop, dead stock identification when items cross the 60-day idle threshold, AR aging alerts when customer collections are deteriorating, supplier quality degradation when rejection rates increase, margin erosion when deal margins fall below category thresholds, warehouse capacity utilisation warnings, overdue invoice collection alerts, and design project pipeline health analysis, among others.

The assistant can create purchase orders through the chat interface. When a user types a purchase order request — "create a PO for Jaquar for 50 units of Flora basin mixer at 4200 per unit" — the AI processes the request, presents a confirmation card with all order details, and creates the PO in the system on user confirmation. This is the Act mode in practice: the AI does not just advise, it executes.

The AI streams its responses in real time, character by character, providing the experience of a conversation with a knowledgeable analyst rather than waiting for a batch response.

No SQL query. No Excel export. No report to pull. No IT team involved. The answer to any business question is available in the moment it is needed.

# SEGMENT 8 — FINANCE AND GST COMPLIANCE (17:45 – 19:00)

The Sales Invoices module provides fully GST-compliant invoicing for all sales transactions. Every invoice created in InvenIQ automatically determines the correct GST treatment based on the customer's GSTIN and the company's registered state.

For customers in the same state, the system applies CGST and SGST at equal halves of the applicable GST rate. For customers in a different state, it applies IGST at the full rate. This determination happens automatically at the invoice level, with no manual intervention required. The risk of applying the wrong GST treatment — a persistent source of compliance liability in manually managed businesses — is completely eliminated.

Invoices are structured with company header (GSTIN, registered address, and bank details configured in Company Profile), customer GSTIN and billing address, line items with HSN codes, quantity, rate, discount, and the GST split per line and in the invoice total. The invoice is formatted for professional printing and email delivery.

Payment tracking is built in. Each invoice records payments received against it, maintains the outstanding balance, and tracks the due date. Invoices approaching their due date and invoices past their due date are flagged separately. The finance team can see the complete accounts receivable position at any time: total outstanding, current, 30–60 days overdue, 61–90 days, and 90-plus days overdue.

The Reports and MIS module provides five report tabs that together cover the complete information requirements of a finance team.

The Sales Report shows revenue by period, product category, and customer. It supports custom date range selection.

The GST Summary Report consolidates all GST output liability by rate and type — CGST, SGST, IGST — for any selected period. This report is structured to match the format required for GST filing, reducing the time required to prepare the return from hours to minutes.

The Purchase Report shows procurement spend by period, supplier, and category, with period-on-period comparison.

The Accounts Receivable Aging Report shows every outstanding customer balance categorised by aging bucket: Current (not yet due), 1 to 30 days overdue, 31 to 60 days, 61 to 90 days, and 90-plus days. This is the primary tool for collections management.

The Stock Summary Report shows current inventory value by category and warehouse location, with days of stock on hand at current sales velocity.

All five reports export to CSV with one click. A chartered accountant can generate every report required for monthly compliance and management review without requesting data from an IT or operations team.

# SUPPORTING INFORMATION — CLIENT Q&A

**"How long does implementation take?"**
For a standard setup: 2 to 3 working days. This includes data migration from existing Excel files or accounting software, system configuration, user setup with role-based access, and team training. The platform is operational within the first week.

**"Is our data secure?"**
The platform runs on the company's own server or private cloud. Business data never leaves the company's infrastructure. There is no SaaS data sharing, no vendor access to business records, and no cloud dependency for the core operation.

**"How many users can access it?"**
Unlimited users. Role-based access controls ensure each team member sees only what is relevant to their function. A salesperson sees quotations and customer data. A warehouse manager sees inventory and procurement. A designer or architect sees only the Design Quote Studio. The CFO sees everything in finance. The business owner sees all 37 modules.

**"What does it cost?"**
Pricing is based on the specific requirements of the business — number of users, number of locations, data migration complexity. The right approach is a 20-minute conversation to understand the setup before providing a number that is accurate and relevant.

**"Can it integrate with Tally?"**
Yes. The Tally Prime Export module generates export files in the exact format required by Tally Prime for stock items, customer ledgers, supplier ledgers, sales vouchers, and purchase vouchers. Finance teams and chartered accountants can import data directly into Tally without any manual re-entry.

**"Does it need an internet connection?"**
The application runs on the local server within the office network. It does not require an internet connection for standard operations. An internet connection is only required for the AI Assistant features, which connect to the GPT-4o model for analysis and recommendations.

**"What happens if the database is not set up yet?"**
The platform runs in Demo Mode without any database configuration. All 37 modules show realistic sample data for a Bangalore building materials dealer. This means the team can evaluate and learn the platform before any data migration begins.
