## WHAT IS INVENIQ

InvenIQ is an AI-powered business operating system built specifically for distributors, trading companies, interior designers, and B2B sales teams in the hardware, sanitary ware, building materials, tiles, and plumbing industry. It is not a generic ERP adapted for this sector — it was designed from the ground up with the workflows, terminology, and daily challenges of this industry in mind.

The platform consolidates every aspect of a business operation into a single application: inventory management, procurement, sales quotations, design project management, customer tracking, finance, GST invoicing, and management reporting. Layered on top of all these operational modules is an artificial intelligence system that analyses live business data around the clock and delivers actionable intelligence on demand, in plain English, without requiring any technical knowledge from the user.

InvenIQ runs as a web application accessible from any browser. It deploys on a company's own server or private cloud infrastructure, meaning business data never leaves the company's control. It can also run in Demo Mode without any database connection, making it possible to evaluate the full platform immediately with realistic sample data.

The platform currently serves businesses ranging from single-location hardware distributors to multi-location B2B operations with 50-plus active customers, managing product catalogs of hundreds of SKUs across dozens of categories.

## THE PROBLEM INVENIQ SOLVES

The hardware and building materials distribution industry operates with tools that were never designed for it. Sales quotations are tracked in WhatsApp message threads. Purchase orders are managed in Excel files that only one person can access. Inventory counts happen manually once a week or once a month. Customer follow-ups depend entirely on whoever happens to remember to call. At the end of every month, finance teams spend three to five days pulling together reports from disconnected sources to understand what happened to the business.

This fragmentation creates five specific problems that cost businesses real money every month.

First, dead stock accumulation. Because inventory is not continuously monitored against sales velocity, businesses accumulate slow-moving and non-moving products that lock up working capital in warehouse space for months without generating revenue. Most businesses only discover the true extent of this problem during an annual physical audit.

Second, quotation leakage. Sales teams managing 40 to 60 active quotations in WhatsApp and email lose track of follow-up timing. Deals that could have been won expire because no one followed up at the right moment. There is no pipeline visibility for the sales manager, and no early warning when a deal is at risk.

Third, procurement inefficiency. Without a structured procure-to-pay workflow, businesses face duplicate purchase orders, receiving errors, supplier invoice discrepancies, and payments made for goods that were partially rejected on quality grounds.

Fourth, GST compliance risk. Businesses operating across state lines need to apply the correct GST treatment — CGST and SGST for intrastate, IGST for interstate — on every invoice. Manual handling of this creates errors, delayed filings, and compliance liability.

Fifth, management information lag. Business owners and CFOs are making decisions based on reports that are days or weeks out of date, because there is no system that aggregates live data from procurement, inventory, sales, and finance simultaneously.

InvenIQ addresses all five of these problems with a single integrated platform and an AI layer that makes the intelligence continuously available to every user.

## THE 37 OPERATIONAL MODULES

InvenIQ is organized into 37 operational modules across seven functional areas. Each module has its own dedicated view, AI integration, and export capability.

### Overview and Analytics

The Business Overview Dashboard is the daily command centre for business owners and senior managers. It displays total revenue for the current period, stock value across all warehouses, open purchase order value, pending customer collections, and the active sales pipeline — all on a single screen, updated in real time. Every KPI tile on the dashboard is connected to the AI: clicking any number opens the AI assistant with a contextual question pre-loaded and ready to answer.

The Analytics and Business Intelligence module provides trend charts, growth rate analysis, category-level performance breakdowns, and comparative period analysis. Sales velocity by product, gross margin trends, customer acquisition and retention metrics, and supplier fill rate performance are all available in this module.

### Inventory Management

The Stock Intelligence module gives complete SKU-level visibility across the entire product catalog. Every item shows current stock quantity, reorder point, days of stock remaining at current sales velocity, supplier linkage, and category classification. The system continuously monitors stock levels against reorder thresholds and surfaces alerts before stockouts occur.

The Dead Stock and Ageing module automatically identifies every slow-moving and non-moving item in the warehouse. It calculates the exact rupee value of capital locked in idle inventory, categorises the problem by product type and age bracket, and through the AI, recommends specific clearance strategies — discounting, bundling, supplier return, or inter-location transfer. For most businesses, a thorough review of this screen identifies lakhs of rupees in reclaimable working capital.

The Demand Forecasting module uses historical sales patterns, seasonal adjustments, and current order pipeline data to project future demand by SKU and category. Procurement teams use this to determine optimal reorder quantities and reduce both stockout risk and overstock accumulation.

The Inward and Outward module tracks every stock movement — from purchase receiving at the warehouse dock to sales dispatch. It captures shrinkage, records put-away locations, and measures dispatch velocity against committed delivery timelines.

The Warehouse Management module provides multi-location capacity tracking, zone utilisation percentages, GRN activity logs, and stock distribution analysis across all warehouse locations.

The Damage Recording module captures and tracks damaged inventory separately from saleable stock, records the reason for damage, value impact, and tracks supplier claims for damaged goods in transit.

### Sales and Customer Management

The Customer Intelligence module maintains a complete profile of every customer: order history, outstanding balance, days since last order, credit utilisation, payment behaviour pattern, and a computed churn probability score. Sales teams can identify high-risk accounts — customers who are approaching credit limits or who have not ordered in an unusual period — before the problem becomes a collection issue.

The Sales Orders module manages the complete order lifecycle from enquiry through delivery. It tracks every stage: Enquiry, Quotation, Sales Order, Delivery Challan, Proof of Delivery, and Payment. Each stage transition is timestamped, and the AI detects orders that are delayed relative to their committed delivery dates and flags them for the operations team.

The Orders and Fulfilment module provides the operations team's view of active orders — what needs to be picked, packed, and dispatched today. It tracks fulfilment SLA compliance and measures the gap between order date and actual dispatch date.

The Freight Planning module helps the logistics team optimise vehicle loading and delivery routing, track freight costs by route, and analyse the cost per delivery relative to order value.

The Sales Performance module gives the sales team and management a comprehensive view of revenue trends, product mix, customer concentration, and period-on-period growth. It identifies which products and customers are driving growth and which are declining.

The Customer Claims and Rebates module manages all customer claim requests — short supply, damaged goods in delivery, rate discrepancies — through a structured workflow from submission to resolution. It tracks the total claims liability at any point in time and generates the accounting entries for credit notes.

The Discount Calculator module helps sales teams calculate margin-aware pricing with configurable segment slabs, category-level overrides, and margin floor guardrails. It ensures that discounts given to secure deals do not compromise the business below acceptable margin thresholds.

The Sales Return module processes customer returns with a condition split — Good condition, Partially damaged, Fully damaged — and automatically generates the corresponding credit note and accounting entries. Box-to-piece unit conversion is handled automatically for returnable items.

The Scheme Management module tracks promotional schemes, volume incentives, and trade schemes for customers and distribution channel partners.

### Projects and Quotations

The Project Tracker module manages the complete inquiry-to-invoice lifecycle for project-based sales. Each project record tracks the customer, project location, estimated value, current stage — Inquiry, Proposal, Negotiation, Approval, Execution, Closed — win probability, and associated quotations and purchase orders. Sales managers get a pipeline view of all active projects with milestone tracking.

The Quotation Builder is the primary revenue generation tool for hardware, sanitary ware, plumbing, tiles, and building materials sales teams. It is described in detail in its own section below.

The Design Quote Studio is the dedicated module for interior design and architect fee proposals. It is described in its own section below.

### Finance and Compliance

The Profitability and Cash module provides the owner-level financial view: gross margin percentage, cash conversion cycle (Days Inventory Outstanding plus Days Sales Outstanding minus Days Payable Outstanding), GST summary, and overdue receivables breakdown.

The Credit Management module tracks credit limits for all customers, current utilisation against those limits, overdue ageing by bucket (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+ days), and post-dated cheque tracking. High-risk accounts — those above credit limit or with severely overdue balances — are automatically flagged.

The Counter POS module supports walk-in customer billing: product search from catalog, cart building, total calculation, and printable receipt generation. It works with catalog data even when offline.

The Sales Invoices module provides fully GST-compliant invoicing for all sales transactions. It is described in its own section below.

The Reports and MIS module provides five management information report tabs. It is described in its own section below.

### Procurement and P2P

The Supplier and Procurement module tracks all supplier relationships: performance history, lead times, fill rates, quality rejection rates, and outstanding PO value. It identifies suppliers with consistently poor quality or delivery performance for review.

The PO and GRN module is the operational backbone of the procurement function. Purchase Orders are raised, sent to suppliers, and tracked through to receiving. Goods Receipt Notes are created against received POs, with quantity and condition verification. The system prevents over-receiving — a GRN cannot be created against a PO that is already fully received, closed, cancelled, or returned.

The Purchase Requisition module manages the internal approval workflow for procurement requests. Any department can raise a PR specifying what they need, the quantity, and the required date. The PR goes through an approval chain before being converted into a PO. A single PR can be split across multiple suppliers, and the system tracks partial conversion per line item.

The QC Inspection module handles the quality inspection stage after goods are received. Each inspection record has four outcome categories: Accepted, Rejected (returned to vendor), Rework Required, and Hold for Further Review. Rejected quantities are automatically deducted from available inventory. The system maintains a quality scorecard per supplier.

The Invoice Matching module performs automated 3-way matching: Purchase Order quantity and rate, Goods Receipt Note received quantity, and supplier invoice. Discrepancies within a one percent tolerance are auto-approved. Larger discrepancies are flagged for manual review. Payment cannot be approved if the QC inspection is still pending for that GRN.

The Landing Cost module captures freight, insurance, handling, and customs charges at GRN level to calculate the true landed cost per unit — essential for accurate margin analysis on imported goods or goods sourced from distant suppliers.

The Distributor Portal module provides a dedicated view for channel partners, showing their allocated stock, order history, credit position, and scheme eligibility.

### Integrations and Utilities

The Tally Prime Export module generates export files in the exact format required by Tally Prime — stock items, customer ledgers, supplier ledgers, sales vouchers, and purchase vouchers. Finance teams and chartered accountants can import data directly into Tally without any manual re-entry.

The AI Assistant is the intelligence layer of the entire platform and is described in its own section below.

## THE QUOTATION BUILDER — DEEP DIVE

The Quotation Builder is the most-used module in InvenIQ for B2B hardware and sanitary ware businesses. It manages every stage of the quotation lifecycle with features built specifically for the challenges of this industry.

The hero section at the top of the Quotation Builder shows seven live key performance indicators simultaneously: total pipeline value across all active quotes, revenue won year-to-date, value lost in the same period, current win rate percentage, average gross margin across the active portfolio, average deal size, and the number of quotations expiring within the next seven days that need urgent follow-up. Each of these tiles is clickable and opens the AI with a contextual question.

The status pipeline strip shows live counts for every stage: All, Draft, Sent, Negotiating, Won, Lost, and Expired. One click on any stage filters the entire quotation table to that stage instantly. A sales manager can assess the health of the complete pipeline in under one minute.

Creating a new quotation takes under two minutes. The form captures customer details, project name, validity date, and GST rate. Line items are added from the product catalog with one-click selection. For each line item, the salesperson enters quantity and discount percentage. The system calculates the unit price from the catalog, computes the line margin in real time, and shows a colour-coded margin health bar — green for healthy, amber for marginal, red for below threshold. A deal score at the top of the form gives the salesperson an immediate read on whether this quotation is commercially sound.

The quotation is formatted for professional printing and email delivery automatically. The printed format includes company letterhead (configured in Settings), itemised product list with HSN codes, quantity, rate, discount, GST breakdown, and total payable. It is ready to share with the customer the moment it is created.

For active quotations, every common action is available directly in the table row without opening the full record. A SENT quotation can be moved to Negotiating with one click when the customer responds. Won and Lost status updates are one-click operations that update the pipeline for the entire team in real time.

The most powerful feature of the Quotation Builder is the per-quote AI assistant. Every quotation row has a dedicated AI button. Clicking it on a SENT quotation that has not received a response generates a personalised WhatsApp follow-up message — with the customer's name, the specific quote amount, product details, a professional and conversational tone, and a direct push toward closing the deal. The salesperson pastes this into WhatsApp and sends it in under ten seconds. For a team managing 40 to 60 active quotations, this eliminates the most common reason for deal loss: failure to follow up at the right moment.

The multi-image product search allows salespeople to photograph unknown products from a site visit or a customer reference image, upload multiple photos simultaneously, and receive AI identification of each product with brand name, category, HSN code, and catalog match. This is particularly useful for matching competitor products or identifying products from old site photographs.

The WhatsApp requirement scanner reads a customer's WhatsApp message — even a free-form, informal description of what they need — and extracts a structured product list with quantities, inferred brands, and HSN codes. The extracted list populates the quotation line items directly.

When a quotation is saved to the product catalog, the system automatically infers the correct HSN code, product category, brand, and finish from the product description — saving the catalog administrator the effort of manually entering these fields.

The Kanban view provides a visual pipeline board with each quotation as a card organised by stage. Sales managers use this view for pipeline review meetings to quickly identify bottlenecks and stalled deals.

## THE DESIGN QUOTE STUDIO — DEEP DIVE

The Design Quote Studio is a dedicated module for interior fit-out businesses, architects, and businesses that supply hardware and sanitary ware to residential and commercial fit-out projects. Interior fit-out projects require a completely different quoting workflow from standard distribution sales — room-wise bills of quantities, multi-level client approvals, architect fee structures, and GST treatment specific to design services.

The module has two distinct workflows: Interior Quotations and Architect Fee Proposals.

Interior Quotations are room-wise bills of quantities for fit-out projects. A typical project for a 24-unit residential development has BOQ sections for each bathroom type — Master Bathroom, Common Bathroom, Powder Room, Utility Area — with specific product items for each. The Design Quote Studio builds these BOQs with AI assistance, manages them through a client approval workflow, and generates the final quotation document.

Each BOQ section lists items with their item type classification — CP fittings (chrome plated), sanitary ware, bathroom accessories, hardware hinges, hardware channels, hardware handles, hardware locks, plumbing, tiles, waterproofing, or installation — along with quantity, unit, rate, and GST at 18% under SAC code 998331 as applicable to design services.

The AI WhatsApp Scanner for Design Quote Studio reads a client's WhatsApp message or site supervisor's notes and automatically identifies every product mentioned: Jaquar Flora basin mixer, Hettich Quadro soft-close channels, Nitco 600×600 porcelain tiles, Pidilite Dr. Fixit waterproofing membrane. For each item the AI extracts the quantity, brand, model reference, inferred HSN code, item type category, number of units, and number of bathrooms per unit for multi-unit residential projects.

A project for Brigade Lakefront — 24 units, 3BHK — could have a complete master bathroom BOQ with 15 to 20 line items extracted automatically from a WhatsApp conversation with the architect. Work that previously took an experienced designer two to three hours is completed in under one minute.

The multi-image visual search in Design Quote Studio allows the team to photograph existing site installations, product specification sheets, or reference images from a client's mood board and have the AI identify each visible product with brand, model, category, and catalog match. Multiple photographs can be processed simultaneously in a two-panel interface: a thumbnail strip for the uploaded images and a results panel showing the AI identification for each.

The QB to DQB sync feature allows a sales quotation created in the Quotation Builder to be imported directly into the Design Quote Studio with one click. This is useful when a distributor's sales team creates an initial product quote and then passes it to the design team for enhancement into a full BOQ.

Architect Fee Proposals follow the standard six-phase project billing structure: Concept Design, Schematic Design, Design Development, Construction Documents, Bidding and Negotiation, and Site Supervision. For each phase, the fee percentage and deliverables are specified. The total architect fee typically ranges from five to eight percent of the total project cost. Each phase can be approved and invoiced independently as the project progresses.

Design quotations go through a structured multi-level approval chain. The architect or designer creates and submits a quote. The sales manager reviews and approves at Level 1. For high-value projects, it escalates to the CFO at Level 2, and to the business owner at Level 3. Every level is handled within the application — no email chains, no WhatsApp forwards, no printed approval forms. Approvers see the full quotation detail inline in the table and can approve, escalate, or return to draft with one click.

The Design Quote Studio is accessible to the Architect role — a dedicated access role where the user can only see the Design Quote Studio, Settings, and About modules. This makes it possible to give external architect partners access to the system for their projects without exposing any internal business data.

## THE AI ASSISTANT — DEEP DIVE

The AI Assistant is the intelligence layer of InvenIQ. It is not a support chatbot or a FAQ tool. It is a business analyst that has read and understood every record in the live database — every inventory entry, every sale, every purchase order, every quotation, every customer interaction, every invoice — and can answer any question about the business in plain English, in real time, with data-driven recommendations.

The assistant operates in three modes. Ask mode delivers quick answers: current stock levels, overdue customer balances, quotation status, supplier lead times. Explain mode delivers deep analysis with root cause identification: why dead stock has accumulated in a specific category, why a supplier's quality rejection rate has increased, why a product segment is showing declining margins. Act mode delivers step-by-step execution plans: a clearance strategy for a specific set of dead stock items, a collection escalation plan for a specific overdue customer, a procurement optimisation plan for the next quarter.

The AI has 28 live data tools that it selects from based on the nature of the question. When asked about customer collection, it uses the customer tool and the finance tool simultaneously. When asked about procurement, it combines the supplier tool, the PO and GRN tool, and the purchase requisition tool. When asked about the design project pipeline, it uses the design quote tool alongside the projects tool and the catalog tool.

The knowledge base contains 36 topics covering industry-specific expertise: GST on design services, Tally integration best practices, credit management for distributors, WhatsApp scanner use cases, landing cost calculation methods, and complete explanations of every InvenIQ workflow. Users can ask conceptual questions — "how does the 3-way matching work in invoice verification?" — and receive detailed explanations of the process.

The proactive intelligence engine runs 26 analytical queries continuously and surfaces insights automatically. These include: demand anomaly detection when a high-velocity product's sales suddenly drop, dead stock identification when items cross the 60-day idle threshold, AR aging alerts when customer collections are deteriorating, supplier quality degradation detection when rejection rates increase, margin erosion alerts when deal margins fall below category thresholds, warehouse capacity utilisation warnings, sales invoice overdue collection alerts, and design quote pipeline health analysis.

The assistant can create purchase orders through the chat interface. When a user types a purchase order request — "create a PO for Jaquar for 50 units of Flora basin mixer at 4200 per unit" — the AI processes the request, presents a confirmation card with all order details, and creates the PO in the system on user confirmation. This is the Act mode in practice.

The assistant streams its responses in real time, character by character, providing the experience of a conversation with a knowledgeable business analyst rather than waiting for a batch response.

## SALES INVOICES AND GST COMPLIANCE

The Sales Invoices module provides fully GST-compliant invoicing for all sales transactions. Every invoice created in InvenIQ automatically determines the correct GST treatment based on the customer's GSTIN and the company's registered state.

For customers in the same state, the system applies CGST and SGST at equal halves of the applicable GST rate. For customers in a different state, it applies IGST at the full rate. This determination happens automatically at the invoice level, with no manual intervention required. The risk of applying the wrong GST treatment — a common source of compliance issues in manually managed systems — is completely eliminated.

Invoices are structured with company header (GSTIN, address, bank details configured in Company Profile), customer details, line items with HSN codes, quantity, rate, discount, and the GST split per line and in total, and the total amount payable. The invoice is formatted for professional printing and email delivery.

Payment tracking is built in. Each invoice records payments received against it, maintains the outstanding balance, and tracks the due date. Invoices approaching their due date and invoices past their due date are flagged separately. The finance team can see the complete AR position — total outstanding, current, 30-60 days overdue, 60-90 days overdue, and 90-plus days overdue — at any time through the Reports module.

## MANAGEMENT REPORTS AND MIS

The Reports and MIS module provides five report tabs that together cover the information requirements of a finance team and management team in a trading business.

The Sales Report shows revenue by period, product category, and customer. It supports custom date range selection and exports to CSV for further analysis.

The GST Summary Report consolidates all GST output liability by rate and type (CGST, SGST, IGST) for any period. This report is structured to match the format required for GST filing, reducing the time required to prepare the return.

The Purchase Report shows procurement spend by period, supplier, and category, with a comparison against the previous period.

The Accounts Receivable Aging Report shows every outstanding customer balance categorised by aging bucket: Current (not yet due), 1–30 days overdue, 31–60 days, 61–90 days, and 90-plus days. This report is the primary tool for collections management.

The Stock Summary Report shows current inventory value by category and warehouse location, with days of stock on hand at current sales velocity.

All five reports export to CSV with one click. A chartered accountant or accounts team member can generate every report they need for monthly compliance and management review without requesting data from an IT or operations team.

## AUTHENTICATION, ROLES, AND ACCESS CONTROL

InvenIQ uses a secure JWT-based authentication system with bcrypt password hashing. Every user login issues an access token valid for eight hours and a refresh token valid for seven days. Tokens are automatically rotated on each refresh, and the system handles concurrent users without session conflicts.

Seven user roles are built into the system. The Admin role has access to all 37 modules and system configuration. The Sales Manager role covers all sales, quotation, and customer modules. The CFO role covers all financial modules including invoices, reports, and credit management. The Warehouse Manager role covers inventory, procurement, warehouse, and P2P modules. The Finance Manager role covers financial reporting and invoice modules. The Distributor role provides the channel partner view through the Distributor Portal. The Architect role provides restricted access to the Design Quote Studio only — appropriate for external architect partners who need to manage their own design quotations without accessing the internal business data.

Each role's module access is enforced at both the API level (middleware checks the JWT claim on every request) and the UI level (modules not in the user's allowed list are hidden from the sidebar and blocked from direct URL access).

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE AND DEPLOYMENT

InvenIQ is built on a modern, reliable technology stack. The frontend is a React 18 single-page application with code-splitting and lazy loading — each of the 37 modules loads on demand, keeping initial load time fast. The backend is a FastAPI application in Python, using asynchronous database connections via aiomysql for high-performance query handling. The database is MySQL.

The AI integration uses the OpenAI GPT-4o model with a 60-second timeout and graceful degradation — if the AI service is temporarily unavailable, the system continues operating and provides a clear, user-friendly message rather than an error.

The application runs in Demo Mode when no database connection is configured. In Demo Mode, all 37 modules display realistic sample data for a Bangalore building materials distributor — CP fittings, sanitary ware, tiles, hardware. This makes it possible to evaluate the complete platform immediately without any data migration or configuration work.

Deployment requires a server running Node.js (for the React build) and Python 3.10 or later (for the FastAPI backend). The startup process is a single script that starts both services, confirms database connectivity, and opens the application in a browser. Setup time from a clean server is under 30 minutes.
