**System**

- Full-screen browser: press F11

**InvenIQ**
- Log in as: `admin` / `InvenIQ_Owner@2026`
- Theme: Dark Mode (Settings → Appearance → Dark)

## SEGMENT 6 — PROCUREMENT AND P2P CYCLE
**Time: 12:45 – 14:45**
**Screen: POGRN → Purchase Requisitions**

---

*[Navigate to: POGRN / Purchase Orders.]*

---

> "For businesses managing their own procurement, InvenIQ includes a complete procure-to-pay workflow."

*[Scroll through the PO list slowly.]*

> "The cycle begins with a Purchase Requisition raised by the warehouse or operations team. That requisition is reviewed, approved, and converted into a Purchase Order — with supplier selection, rate negotiation notes, and expected delivery dates."

> "When goods arrive, a Goods Receipt Note is created against the PO. The system enforces receiving controls — a GRN cannot be raised against a PO that is already fully received, closed, or cancelled. This prevents duplicate receiving and double-payment errors."

*[Navigate briefly to QC Inspection if time permits, or continue.]*

> "After receiving, items go through Quality Control inspection — with four outcome categories: Accepted, Rejected, Rework Required, and Hold for Review. Rejected quantities are automatically deducted from stock. Rework items are tracked separately."

> "When the supplier invoice arrives, the system performs a 3-way match — comparing the Purchase Order quantity and rate, the GRN received quantity, and the supplier invoice. Discrepancies are flagged automatically. Payment cannot proceed if QC is still pending or if the quantities do not reconcile."

*[Navigate to: Purchase Requisitions.]*

> "Purchase Requisitions flow to Purchase Orders with one click. If multiple suppliers are involved — one handling fittings, another handling tiles — the system generates separate POs from a single PR and tracks partial conversion per line item. Finance teams get complete traceability from requisition to payment."
